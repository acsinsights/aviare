If you're looking to start a business in the UAE but want complete ownership, minimal bureaucracy, and cost-efficient setup — a Free Zone license may be exactly what you need.

Guide to Free Zone Company Setup in the UAE

  • Determine the business activity and select the most suitable Free Zone jurisdiction.
  • Choose a legal structure such as FZE, FZC, or Branch Office based on ownership needs.
  • Reserve and approve a trade name in compliance with Free Zone regulations.
  • Submit shareholder documents, passport copies, and application forms for initial approval.
  • Lease or obtain a Flexi-desk or office space within the chosen Free Zone.
  • Prepare and sign the Memorandum of Association and other incorporation documents.
  • Obtain necessary approvals and pay the license and registration fees.
  • Collect the trade license and registration certificates upon completion.
  • Open a corporate bank account to facilitate business transactions.
  • Apply for residence visas and establish employee sponsorships under the company.
  • 100% foreign ownership with no local sponsor required.
  • Full repatriation of profits and capital.
  • Tax exemptions, including corporate and personal income tax benefits (Designated Zones only).
  • Simplified company registration and licensing procedures.
  • Strategic access to international markets and global business networks.
  • Flexible office solutions including virtual offices and co-working spaces.
  • Quick issuance of trade license and streamlined regulatory approvals.
  • Eligibility to sponsor employees and obtain residence visas.
  • Tailored business support and facilities provided by Free Zone authorities.
  • Ideal for import/export, trading, and service-oriented and international businesses.
  • Passport copies of all shareholders and directors.
  • Visa copies of UAE residents (if applicable).
  • Completed application forms provided by the Free Zone authority.
  • Proof of business activity (e.g., business plan or activity description).
  • Proposed company trade name reservation certificate.
  • Shareholders' and directors' passport-sized photographs.
  • Bank reference letter or proof of financial standing (if requested).
  • Special approvals or licenses for regulated activities (if required by the Free Zone).
  • Choose the appropriate free zone based on your business activity, budget, and visa requirements.
  • Select and reserve your company name according to the free zone's naming policies.
  • Choose your office space option (flexi-desk, dedicated desk, or private office).
  • Prepare and submit all required documentation to the free zone authority.
  • Obtain initial approvals and complete lease agreement for office space.
  • Receive trade license and company registration documents.
  • Apply for investor and employee visas as needed.
  • Open corporate bank account and complete any additional setup requirements.
